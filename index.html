Tip #1: Gather Safe Materials

-- Pay attention to what you burn! Make sure you burn wood that is DRY and UNTREATED only. Logs 6 inches or smaller in diameter work best and safest.
-- Gather your long shovel, rake, or poker; fire tongs; and fire pit screen for safest campfire use (links to products in menu bar).

Tip #2: Safe Placement

-- Place covered fire pits and chimneys (safest option) 10 feet away from buildings, shrubs, or anything that can burn.
-- Place open fire pits 25 feet away from buildings, shrubs, or anything that can burn. Line open fire pits with bricks or cement blocks.
-- Never place a fire under tree branches, awnings, overhangs, or anything else that may burn. Above your fire should be open sky.


Tip #3: Check the Weather

-- Windy and excessively dry weather can increase chances of fire and sparks spreading. Choose a day with little wind and no recent drought.

Tip #4: Know your emergency numbers.

-- 911 is always available but check numbers for the local fire departments, hospitals, and ambulance systems as well. Keep the numbers and a cell phone on      hand, but safely away from the fire.
